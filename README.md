# DKNS Tea Lands

A web app for managing tea estate employees, attendance, harvest collection, and payroll.

## Features

- **Employees** — digital profiles (personal + employment details), auto-generated employee IDs, printable QR check-in badges
- **Attendance** — check-in/out, breaks, status, verification method; auto-computed work hours. An **Attendance Summary** panel (scoped to the current weekly pay cycle) shows Total Days Worked, Days Absent, No-Pay (LOP) Days, Paid Leave Days, Attendance Percentage, Total Working Hours, and Overtime Hours, plus a per-employee breakdown with a leave balance and a red "Under hours" flag for anyone who hasn't completed their required daily/weekly hours (set per employee). **Leave is integrated into payroll**: Sick Leave is always paid, and annual Leave is paid only up to each employee's configured entitlement for the year — once that's used up, further Leave days count as No-Pay, the same as Absences, and don't add anything to pay; paid leave days are compensated at the employee's day rate (`required_daily_hours × hourly_rate`)
- **Work & Harvest** — daily task assignments with a harvest target; weighing entries automatically update output and productivity (`Actual_Output += Net_Weight`, `Productivity = Actual_Output / Target`); non-harvest tasks (Fertilizer/Pruning/Weeding/Spraying) can be marked Pending/In Progress/Completed manually. Each employee can have an over-target commission % — kg up to the target are paid at the normal rate/kg, and kg beyond the target earn that commission as a bonus on top of the rate (e.g. target 25kg, rate 50, 20% commission, 29kg actual → 25×50 + 4×50×1.20 = 1490)
- **Payroll** — automatic weekly pay cycles (Monday–Sunday, based on the Sri Lankan calendar and Asia/Colombo time), with wages due the following Monday. The Payroll page always shows the current cycle's dates, due date, and status, and computes harvest-based pay (`kg × rate/kg`) and time-based pay (`hours × hourly rate`) live from that week's attendance and harvest data. Salary advances can be recorded per employee/date and are shown as a separate Advance column with a computed Net Pay (`Total Pay − Advance`). Employees can opt in to **EPF/ETF** (Sri Lanka statutory rates): 8% is deducted from the employee's pay, while the employer separately contributes 12% EPF + 3% ETF. **Marking a cycle as Paid** freezes every employee's figures into a permanent snapshot (so later edits to attendance/harvest can never retroactively change a paid cycle's numbers), locks it as read-only, removes it from the active Payroll page, and automatically opens the next Monday–Sunday cycle. Payslips can only be generated for the *current* unpaid cycle from the Payroll page; once a cycle is paid, its payslips move to **Payroll History**, where every past cycle can be searched (by date or employee name), viewed, reprinted, and exported to CSV or a printable PDF report (with a Prepared-by/Authorized-by signature section and page numbers). Each cycle also tracks per-employee **payment method, date, reference/transaction ID, and status** (Pending/Paid/Failed), editable after the fact if a payment needs correcting. Employee profiles store full banking details (Bank Name, Branch, Account Name, Account Number, Branch/BSB Code, Account Type) and a default payment method (Cash, Bank Transfer, Cheque, Other) — shown on payslips and in Payroll History. All payroll dates display in Sri Lankan `DD/MM/YYYY` format
- **QR check-in** — each employee's badge encodes a URL; scanning it with any phone's camera (no app needed) opens a check-in page and logs attendance automatically
- **Login &amp; User Management** — every management page requires an account; the QR check-in and badge pages stay public so employees never need credentials to check in. Two roles: **Admin** (full access, including User Management itself) and **Dhanu Operations** (Dashboard, Employees, Attendance, Work &amp; Harvest only — Payroll, Income &amp; Profit, Finance &amp; Factory and User Management are hidden from the nav and blocked if visited directly). Admins can create, edit, disable/enable, delete, and reset the password of any user from **User Management**. Disabling a user takes effect immediately, even on an account that's already logged in — not just on their next login attempt. The system always keeps at least one active Admin: you can't disable, delete, or demote the last one (including yourself), and you can't disable or delete your own account while logged in
- **Income & Profit** — set the tea selling price for any day (it changes daily); view Daily, Weekly (Mon–Sun), Monthly (calendar month), or a Custom date range. Income sums each day's harvest × *that day's* price (never a single price across a range), and flags any days in the range with no price set instead of guessing. Also shows Cost (employee pay + logged expenses), Profit/Loss, a per-employee income/profit breakdown, and a percentage-of-cost breakdown
- **Expenses** — occasional costs (Fertilizer, Transport, Fuel, Food, Others) logged per date, only added when they actually happen; they fold into that day's total cost and profit calculation
- **Finance & Factory** — manage the factories you deliver tea to, record each delivery (estate weight auto-fills from that day's harvest weighings, factory weight entered manually so any shortfall is visible), then bundle un-invoiced deliveries in a date range into an invoice at a chosen price/kg. Track payments (Cash/Cheque/Bank Transfer) against each invoice — status moves from Unpaid → Partially Paid → Paid automatically as payments are recorded/removed. A Daily/Weekly/Monthly/Custom dashboard shows revenue invoiced, payments received, outstanding receivables, payroll + expense cost, and net profit/loss, with a feed of recent invoices
- **Finance & Factory Statement** — a single chronological ledger (like a bank statement) of every financial event: expenses, payroll cost (only once a pay cycle is actually processed and marked Paid — including the employer's EPF/ETF contribution), salary advances, factory deliveries, invoices, invoice payments, and depreciation/amortization. **Revenue is recognized only when a factory payment is actually received** — not when tea is harvested and not merely when an invoice is raised — and **payroll only becomes a ledger expense once its weekly cycle is marked Paid on its Monday pay date**, matching when the cash actually moves. Each row shows Date, Reference No., Type, Description, Debit, Credit, a running Balance, Payment Method, and the User who recorded it. Filter by date range (Daily/Weekly/Monthly/Custom), transaction type, factory, employee, or invoice/payment status; search by description or reference number; sort by any column. Shows Opening and Closing balance for the selected range (correctly carried forward from all earlier history, even under the active filters), and exports to CSV or a print-ready PDF (A4 portrait or landscape) with the company header, statement period, page numbers, totals, and a signature section — the PDF mirrors the on-screen table exactly. Deliveries and invoices appear in the ledger for traceability but don't move the balance themselves (debit/credit = 0) — only the actual invoice *payment* is real cash inflow, avoiding double-counting the same money as it goes from delivered → invoiced → paid
- **Depreciation & Amortization** (under Finance) — Straight-line depreciation for registered assets (`(Purchase Cost − Salvage Value) / Depreciation Period` per month) and amortization for standalone prepaid expenses, with a selectable period (Monthly/Quarterly/Yearly). Example: fertilizer bought for a 3-month supply automatically expenses one third of its cost each month. Both post automatically, one entry per elapsed period, into the Finance & Factory Statement as a "Depreciation" transaction type — no manual journal entries needed
- **Asset & Equipment Register** (Admin only) — Asset ID (auto-generated, e.g. `AST-0001`), Name, Category, Purchase Date/Cost, Salvage Value, Supplier, Serial Number, Assigned Location/Employee, Warranty Expiry, Service Schedule, and Status (Active/Under Repair/Disposed). Each asset's detail page shows live book value, accumulated depreciation, and monthly depreciation once a depreciation period is set, plus a maintenance log (date, description, cost, performed by, next service date)
- **Inventory & Stock Management** — track Fertilizer, Chemicals, Packaging Materials, Field Supplies, and Other stock by item: Stock In/Out transactions (with Supplier, Batch Number, Expiry Date), running Current Balance, Minimum Stock Level with a "Low" flag when balance drops below it, and Stock Valuation (quantity × latest unit cost). Stock-Out transactions can't exceed the current balance. Accessible to both Admin and Dhanu Operations, since it's day-to-day field/estate work
- **User Login History** (Admin only, under User Management) — every login attempt (success or failure) is recorded automatically with username, date, time, logout time, IP address, and device/browser, filterable by username, status, and date
- **7-Day Weather Forecast** — live forecast for Galle, Sri Lanka from Open-Meteo (no API key needed): current conditions plus 7 days of high/low, rain chance, rainfall, and wind, shown at `/weather` and summarized on the Dashboard. Cached in the database for 45 minutes at a time (Vercel's serverless filesystem can't hold an in-memory cache), falling back to the last cached forecast if Open-Meteo is temporarily unreachable
- **Employee Birthday Reminders & Auto Updates** — the Employees page shows an "Upcoming Birthdays" widget (next 30 days, with a same-day highlight), and each employee's detail page shows their own next-birthday countdown. The Dashboard's **Auto Updates** panel auto-refreshes today's tea price, the current Galle weather, upcoming birthdays, and admin-posted announcements (Admins can post/delete announcements; everyone can read them)

## Requirements

- Python 3.10+
- Dependencies in `requirements.txt` (Flask, qrcode, Pillow, reportlab, psycopg2-binary, tzdata)

## Setup

```bash
pip install -r requirements.txt
python app.py
```

The app creates `tea_estate.db` (SQLite) in this folder on first run — no separate database setup needed.

Visit `http://localhost:5000` on the machine running it. The first visit will prompt you to create the admin account (username + password) — there are no default/hardcoded credentials.

## Using it from other devices (phones, tablets)

The server binds to `0.0.0.0`, so other devices on the same WiFi/LAN can reach it:

1. Find the host machine's LAN IP (Windows: `ipconfig`, look for the IPv4 address on your WiFi/Ethernet adapter).
2. From another device on the same network, visit `http://<that-ip>:5000`.
3. **Windows Firewall**: the first time, Windows will likely prompt "Windows Defender Firewall has blocked some features of this app" — click **Allow access** (at least for Private networks), or other devices won't be able to connect.
4. Print employee QR badges *after* accessing the app via the LAN IP (not `localhost`) — the badge encodes whatever address you used to load the page, so it needs to be the address other phones can actually reach.

## Running the tests

```bash
pip install -r requirements-dev.txt
python -m pytest tests/ -v
```

Tests use a temporary SQLite database (never touches your real `tea_estate.db`) and cover auth, employee CRUD, attendance, the harvest weighing math from the spec, payroll calculations, the QR check-in flow, the Finance & Factory module (factory CRUD, delivery/invoice/payment flows, dashboard totals), User Management (role-based access restrictions, disabling a live session immediately, last-admin/self-lockout safety guards), the Finance & Factory Statement (every transaction type appears correctly, running/opening/closing balance math, all filter combinations, search, sorting, and CSV/PDF export content), and the weekly Payroll cycle system (current-cycle display, mark-paid snapshot freezing and locking, automatic next-cycle creation, Payroll History search/filtering, payment status editing, and banking details on payslips) — including regression tests for bugs found during development (employee-number collisions after deletes, stale-edit conflicts, `None` leaking into form values). Payroll tests compute the current Monday–Sunday cycle dynamically (matching the app's own Asia/Colombo logic) rather than hardcoding dates, so they stay correct regardless of which real-world week they run in. Also covers the Attendance Summary (days worked/absent/paid-leave/No-Pay counts, attendance percentage, overtime, leave balance, under-hours highlighting) and its integration into payroll's pay calculation, plus the seven newer modules: Login History (success/failure recording, logout timestamps, admin-only access, filtering), Inventory (stock in/out math, overdraw protection, low-stock flagging, valuation), Asset Register (auto-generated asset codes, straight-line depreciation math, maintenance logging, admin-only access), Depreciation (prepaid-expense amortization matching the spec's fertilizer example, ledger integration, admin-only access), Weather (parsing and caching, with `urlopen` mocked so the suite never depends on live network access), and Birthdays & Announcements (upcoming-birthday windowing, dashboard Auto Updates rendering, and announcement post/delete permissions). A global `conftest.py` fixture blocks real network calls by default (Open-Meteo would otherwise be hit on every dashboard-touching test), so the whole suite runs fast and offline.

## What's not built yet

These need real hardware/decisions before they can be implemented:

- **Face-recognition check-in** — needs a camera device and a face-matching service; QR check-in covers the same need today with zero extra hardware.
- **IoT smart scale integration** — weighing is currently manual entry (staff type in gross/tare weight). Once you have a scale, tell me how it outputs data (Bluetooth, WiFi/API, USB/serial) and it can be wired in to replace manual entry.

## Known limitations

- Two fixed roles (Admin, Dhanu Operations) rather than granular per-page permissions — fine for a small estate's office + field-operations split, not meant as a general-purpose permissions system.
- Optimistic concurrency prevents two simultaneous edits from silently overwriting each other, but there's no true multi-user support (audit log, per-user attribution).
- Uses Flask's built-in development server (`debug=True`) — fine for a single estate's internal use, but not hardened for public internet exposure.
- Sessions use `app.secret_key` from the `SECRET_KEY` environment variable, falling back to a hardcoded dev value if it's not set — fine for local/LAN use, but **set a real `SECRET_KEY` in Vercel's Project Settings → Environment Variables** before relying on this in production, or every server restart invalidates everyone's login session anyway.
- **Only run one `python app.py` at a time locally.** SQLite only allows one writer; if you start it twice (e.g. from two terminals) you'll get `database is locked` errors. Stop a running instance with Ctrl+C before starting another, rather than opening a second terminal on top of it. (This doesn't apply on Vercel — see below, it uses Postgres there instead.)
- EPF/ETF percentages (`EPF_EMPLOYEE_PERCENT`, `EPF_EMPLOYER_PERCENT`, `ETF_EMPLOYER_PERCENT` in `utils.py`) are hardcoded at the current standard Sri Lanka statutory rates (8% / 12% / 3%). If the government changes these rates, update the constants in `utils.py` — they apply to every EPF/ETF-enabled employee automatically.
- The company address/phone/email shown on the printed Finance & Factory Statement (`COMPANY_ADDRESS`, `COMPANY_PHONE`, `COMPANY_EMAIL` in `app.py`) are placeholders — there's no company-profile settings page. Set them via environment variables of the same name, or edit the constants directly, before handing out a printed statement.
- "User" attribution on ledger rows only exists for transactions recorded *after* this feature was added (each source table gained a `created_by` column) — anything created earlier shows "—" for User, since there's no way to know who entered it retroactively.
- The first payroll cycle is auto-created for whichever real-world Monday–Sunday week the Payroll page is first opened in — there's no backfill of earlier weeks. Pay cycles only advance when you mark the current one as Paid; if you skip weeks, the "current" cycle stays the oldest unpaid one rather than silently jumping ahead, so nothing gets lost.
- Requires the `tzdata` package (pinned in `requirements.txt`) for the Asia/Colombo timezone lookup to work — this matters most on Windows, where Python's `zoneinfo` has no built-in system tz database and raises `ZoneInfoNotFoundError` without it.
- Leave policy is a fixed, simple model rather than a configurable rules engine: Sick Leave is always paid; annual Leave is paid only up to each employee's `annual_leave_entitlement` (days/year, set on their profile) — once exhausted, further Leave days are No-Pay. The entitlement year always resets on 1 January, and leave usage is tracked as a simple running count, not carried over or accrued monthly. "Overtime" and "Under hours" are based on a single `required_daily_hours` figure per employee — there's no separate weekly-hours target or shift-pattern support.
- Inventory valuation uses **last cost** (each item's unit cost is overwritten by its most recent Stock-In transaction), not a weighted average or FIFO — fine for a single-estate operation with simple pricing, but it means valuation can swing with the price of the most recent purchase.
- The weather forecast is fixed to Galle, Sri Lanka (hardcoded coordinates in `app.py`) — there's no multi-location support or a settings page to change it. If the estate is elsewhere, update `GALLE_LATITUDE`/`GALLE_LONGITUDE`.
- The "Daily Tea Market Price" shown in Auto Updates is the price you set yourself on the Income page (today's, or the most recent one on file) — it's not pulled from an external tea-auction market feed.
- Announcements are simple (message + timestamp + author), with no scheduling, expiry, or per-role targeting — every active announcement is shown to every logged-in user, and only Admins can post or remove one.

## Database connection handling

`database.get_connection()` caches one connection per request on Flask's `g`, and `app.py` registers `close_connection` via `app.teardown_appcontext()`. Flask guarantees teardown callbacks run even when a request raises an unhandled exception — so a crash can never leave a connection (and its lock) dangling. This replaced an earlier pattern of manually calling `conn.close()` at the end of each route, which silently leaked the connection (and the lock) whenever a route crashed before reaching that line.

## Deploying to Vercel

Vercel's serverless functions have no writable, persistent local disk — every invocation gets its own throwaway filesystem — so the local SQLite file (`tea_estate.db`) cannot be used there; the very first request would crash with `sqlite3.OperationalError: unable to open database file`. To fix this, `database.py` now runs in one of two modes:

- **Locally / in tests**: no Postgres connection string is set in the environment, so it uses SQLite exactly as before — nothing changes for local development.
- **On Vercel**: once a Postgres connection string is present in the environment (`POSTGRES_URL`, `DATABASE_URL`, or `POSTGRES_URL_NON_POOLING`), it switches to Postgres automatically. A small compatibility layer (`PGConnection` in `database.py`) lets every existing route keep using the same `conn.execute("... WHERE id = ?", (x,))` style calls unchanged — no route code had to change.

**One-time setup, in the Vercel dashboard:**

1. Open your project → **Storage** tab → **Create Database** → choose **Postgres** (Neon-backed).
2. Connect it to this project. Vercel automatically adds a `POSTGRES_URL` (or `DATABASE_URL`) environment variable — no manual copy/paste needed.
3. Still in **Project Settings → Environment Variables**, add one more: `SECRET_KEY` set to any long random string (used to sign login sessions).
4. Redeploy (or just push a commit) — the next cold start runs `init_db()` automatically and creates all tables on the new Postgres database.
5. Visit the site and go through **Create admin account** again — it's a fresh database, so the SQLite admin account you created locally doesn't carry over.

Everything else (QR codes, CSV export, PDF payslips/invoices) is generated in-memory and was already Vercel-compatible; the database was the only local-file dependency.

## Recovering the DKNS Super Admin account

The `DKNS` account is the permanent Super Admin / System Owner (see [Login &amp; User Management](#features) above) — by design, once it exists, no one else (not even another Admin) can reach User Management, disable it, delete it, or change its role, and the app itself has no "forgot password" flow for it.

If you're ever locked out of DKNS specifically (unknown/lost password on a fresh deployment, for example), that's the one legitimate case for bypassing the app directly: run `scripts/reset_super_admin.py` from your own machine against your own production database. It prompts for a new password (hidden input, never typed into chat or committed anywhere) and creates the DKNS account if it doesn't exist yet, or resets its password if it does — using the exact same password hashing the app already uses, so it works immediately with the normal login form. See the comment at the top of that script for exact steps. This intentionally requires your own Postgres connection string (from Vercel → Storage → your database), which only you have access to — there is no way to reset this account from within the running app itself, since that would defeat the point of it being protected.
